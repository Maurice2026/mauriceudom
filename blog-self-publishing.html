Self-Publishing: A Complete Guide for First-Time Authors

From finished manuscript to published book — everything you need to know to publish your first book, anywhere in the world.

You have written a book. That alone puts you ahead of 95% of people who say they want to write one. Now comes the question every first-time author faces: how do I actually get this published?

Twenty years ago, the answer was simple and brutal — find a traditional publisher or give up. Today, self-publishing has transformed the landscape. Authors in Nigeria, India, the Philippines, the United Kingdom, and everywhere between can publish professionally and reach readers worldwide. This guide walks you through the entire process, step by step.

Traditional Publishing vs Self-Publishing

In traditional publishing, a publisher accepts your manuscript, handles editing, design, printing, and distribution, and pays you royalties — typically 7–15% of the cover price. The catch? Getting accepted can take years of rejection, and you give up creative control.

In self-publishing, you handle the process yourself (or hire professionals to help), keep full creative control, and earn 35–70% royalties depending on the platform. The catch? You are responsible for quality, marketing, and distribution. Done well, self-publishing is faster, more profitable, and more empowering than the traditional route.

01
Finish and Polish Your Manuscript

Before you think about publishing, your manuscript must be complete and polished. This means finishing the entire draft, revising it at least twice yourself, and then getting professional editing. Do not skip this step. The single biggest mistake first-time authors make is publishing a manuscript that has not been properly edited.

There are different levels of editing. Developmental editing examines structure, argument, plot, and pacing. Line editing polishes sentence-level clarity and style. Proofreading catches spelling, punctuation, and formatting errors. At minimum, every book needs proofreading. Ideally, it needs all three.

Tip: If you cannot afford a professional editor, find a trusted colleague, teacher, or fellow writer who reads carefully and will give you honest feedback. 02
Choose Your Publishing Platform

Several platforms allow you to publish and sell your book globally. Each has different strengths depending on your audience and goals.

Amazon KDP

The world's largest book marketplace. Publish eBooks and print-on-demand paperbacks. Available globally. 35–70% royalties on eBooks.

Selar

Popular in Africa. Sell eBooks, PDFs, and digital products directly. Accept payments in local currencies including Naira, Cedis, and Rand. Up to 97% of the sale price.

IngramSpark

Professional distribution to bookstores and libraries worldwide. Higher setup costs but wider reach for print books.

Gumroad / Payhip

Simple platforms for selling eBooks and digital downloads directly to readers. Good for building a direct audience.

You do not have to choose just one. Many successful authors publish on Amazon KDP for global reach and on Selar or Gumroad for direct sales where they keep a higher percentage.

03
Design a Professional Cover

Readers judge books by their covers. This is not a metaphor — it is a documented fact of book-buying behaviour. A poorly designed cover signals an amateur book, no matter how brilliant the writing inside.

If you have the budget, hire a professional cover designer. Platforms like Fiverr and 99designs offer affordable options starting from $30–$100. If you are designing it yourself, use Canva (which has book cover templates) and study the covers of bestselling books in your genre. Notice the fonts, colours, and layouts that work — then follow those patterns.

Your cover must look good as a small thumbnail, because that is how most readers will first see it — as a tiny image in a search result or online bookstore.

04
Format Your Book Properly

Formatting is the process of turning your Word document into a properly structured book file. For eBooks, you need an EPUB or MOBI file. For print books, you need a correctly sized PDF with proper margins, page numbers, headers, and chapter breaks.

Free tools like Reedsy Book Editor and Calibre can handle eBook formatting. For print, Amazon KDP provides templates you can download and use in Microsoft Word. If formatting feels overwhelming, services like Reedsy and Fiverr offer professional formatting at reasonable prices.

Key formatting details to get right: consistent fonts throughout, proper paragraph spacing, page numbers, a table of contents, a title page, and a copyright page.

05
Write a Compelling Book Description

Your book description is your sales pitch. On Amazon and other platforms, it is the single most important factor — after the cover — in convincing someone to buy. Yet most first-time authors write bland, generic descriptions that tell readers nothing compelling.

A strong book description does three things: it hooks the reader with a provocative opening line, it sets up the central conflict or promise of the book, and it ends with a reason to buy now. For fiction, focus on the protagonist, the stakes, and the tension. For non-fiction, focus on the problem the book solves and what the reader will gain.

Study the descriptions of bestselling books in your genre. Notice how they are structured — short paragraphs, bold text, and a clear call to action.

06
Set the Right Price

Pricing depends on your genre, audience, format, and goals. Here are general guidelines:

For eBooks, $2.99–$9.99 is the standard range on Amazon (the 70% royalty rate applies within this range). For print books, price based on your production cost plus a reasonable margin — Amazon KDP shows you the minimum price based on page count. For markets in Africa, Asia, and Latin America, platforms like Selar allow you to price in local currency, which removes the barrier of international payment for local readers.

If you are a first-time author with no audience, consider launching at a lower price to attract initial readers and reviews, then raising the price once you have social proof.

07
Market Your Book

Publishing your book is only half the work. Without marketing, even a brilliant book will sit unsold. Here are the most effective free marketing strategies for self-published authors:

Build an author website or blog — a central place where readers can learn about you and your work. Use social media strategically — share excerpts, behind-the-scenes content, and reader testimonials. Ask for reviews — send free copies to bloggers, book reviewers, and friends who will leave honest reviews on Amazon. Join writing communities — engage with other authors and readers on Goodreads, Reddit, and Facebook groups. Build an email list — collect email addresses and notify subscribers when you publish new work.

The authors who sell consistently are not necessarily the best writers — they are the ones who show up, engage with their audience, and keep their work visible.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Publishing before the book is ready. Rushing to publish an unedited manuscript is the fastest way to earn bad reviews and damage your reputation as an author.

2. Designing your own cover without design skills. A bad cover costs you far more in lost sales than a professional designer would cost.

3. Setting the wrong price. Too high and no one buys. Too low and readers question the quality. Research your genre and price accordingly.

4. Doing no marketing at all. "If I build it, they will come" does not work in publishing. You must actively promote your book.

5. Giving up after low initial sales. Most self-published books sell slowly at first. Consistency, marketing, and building a catalogue over time is what creates success.

Self-publishing is not a shortcut — it is a different path. It requires the same commitment to quality as traditional publishing, plus the willingness to learn skills in design, marketing, and distribution. But the reward is full creative control, higher royalties, and the ability to reach readers anywhere in the world on your own terms.

The best time to publish your book was five years ago. The second best time is now.
